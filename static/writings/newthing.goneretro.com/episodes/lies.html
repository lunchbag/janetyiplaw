Lies, Lies, Lies
- - Janet [07/16/01]

Monday, July 16, 2001 was a day of lies.

First of all, as I was playing Spider Solitaire on the computer, Ian messaged me saying that Lahan* said that she was "very good at Spider" and frequently won games at the difficult level.

WELL
No one is smarter than me, and I can only beat the medium level games half of the time. Therefore, Lahan is a liar. If you ever meet anyone named Lahan, they may be compulsive liars, but you would never know because the real person in this story is not named Lahan.

Today, at Ian's house, I started playing Spider Solitaire at the medium level. After winning it on the first try, I started to brag about how I was the only one smart enough to beat Spider at the medium level. Ian then spoke up, saying that he has beaten Spider at the medium level too.

SO
I clicked on the Statistics tab, and it read:

Wins: 1
Losses: 6
Win Rate: 14%

Hahahha point proven.

So Ian protested that he doesn't play on that particular computer, and so, the statistics did not reflect his victories. Ignoring his comment, I opened Minesweeper. It was set to a custom window of 30 x 24 with ten mines. I started to make fun of Ian, and again he said that he doesn't play on that particular computer and so, the settings must have been set by his brother.

SO
I opened Best Times, and the first two scores read "Ian". The last one read "Ina".

Ian's rebuttal was that the scores were from a long time ago.

Overall conclusion: if you lie, I will find you and expose you for the liar that you are! So don't lie.

(Epilogue: Ian called the day 'a bad sequence of events'.)
